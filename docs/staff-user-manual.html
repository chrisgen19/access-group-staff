01 · Welcome

Welcome to Access Recognition

Access Recognition is our internal thank-you app. Whenever a colleague does something that makes your day a bit better — big or small — you can send them a branded recognition card tied to one of our Access Group values.

What makes it special

It's personal

Every card is addressed to a specific person and explains exactly what they did. No generic "great job" — you say why.

It's on-brand

Cards use the Access Group visual identity, so a thank-you feels like a real moment — not a throwaway chat message.

It's tied to values

Each card calls out one or more of our five values, so recognition reinforces what we stand for as a team.

The Access Group values

You'll tick one or more of these every time you send a card. They're the "why" behind the recognition.

VALUEPeople
VALUESafety
VALUERespect
VALUECommunication
VALUEContinuous
Improvement

Who this guide is for

This handbook is written for team members with the STAFF role — that's the default for everyone at Access Group. Managers and administrators have a few extra tools (like staff directory management) that aren't covered here, since they're not available to you.

The golden rule. You can't send a card to yourself — recognition only counts when it's given, not claimed. And please keep it genuine: one thoughtful card a month beats ten empty ones.

02 · Sign In

Signing in for the first time

There are two ways to get into Access Recognition. Most people use their Microsoft work account — that means no extra password to remember.

Option 1 — Continue with Microsoft (recommended)

1

Open the app

Go to access.cgdev.site in your browser. Chrome, Edge, Safari, and Firefox all work.

2

Click Continue with Microsoft

You'll be sent to the familiar Microsoft sign-in page. Use your Access Group email and the password you already use for Outlook and Teams.

3

Approve the sign-in prompt

If Microsoft sends you an authenticator notification, tap Approve. You'll be dropped straight onto your Access Recognition dashboard.

Option 2 — Email & password

If you've been set up with email-and-password access, enter your corporate email and password in the fields below the Microsoft button, then click Sign in.

Can't sign in? If you're brand new to Access Group and your account hasn't been created yet, give HR or your manager a heads-up — your account is added by an administrator, so it'll appear once you're onboarded in the system.

Signing out

To sign out, click the Sign Out button at the bottom of the left-hand sidebar on any dashboard screen. On a shared computer, always sign out before you leave.

04 · Send a Card

Sending a recognition card

Sending a card takes under a minute. There are two steps — Fill Card and Review & Submit — and the preview updates live as you type.

Step 1 — Fill Card

1

Pick a colleague

Click the TO field and start typing their name. Matches appear instantly.

2

Write what they did

In the WHAT YOU DID box, describe the moment. Be specific — "stayed late to help debug the deploy" hits harder than "was awesome".

3

Tick at least one value

On the right, select every Access Group value the person demonstrated. You must pick at least one; multiple is fine.

4

Check the date

It defaults to today. You can backdate a card if you're recognising something that happened earlier — but future dates are blocked, so the form won't let you pick tomorrow or later.

Step 2 — Review & Submit

Click Review Before Submit. The card flips to its final, on-brand look so you can double-check everything before it goes out.

Once you send:

  • The recipient gets a notification in their bell icon.
  • The card appears in your Sent tab and their Received tab.
  • If the card is public, it also shows up in the recognition feed.
You can edit your own cards. If you spot a typo after sending, open the card from your Sent tab and click Edit. Recipients will see an "Edited" notification so they know you tidied it up.

05 · Your Inbox

Your inbox — Received & Sent

Click Recognition Card in the sidebar to open your personal inbox. Two tabs: cards you've been given, and cards you've sent.

Received

Each row is a full, clickable recognition card. Click anywhere on the card to open it in full view, where you can see reactions and comments. See Section 6 for the full card view.

Sent

From the Sent view, you can:

  • Click any card to open and share it.
  • Click Edit on a card you authored to fix the message or adjust the values.
  • Leave the same card and come back later — the list is always fresh.
The red "9+" badge next to Recognition Card in the sidebar is your unread counter. Once you've opened a card, it's marked as read and the number drops.

06 · Opening a Card

Opening a card

Clicking any card — in your inbox, in the public feed, or via a shared link — opens it in full. This is where the card really shines: reactions, comments, and the Access Group front-of-card design.

The front of the card

Flip it over

The Flip to front / Flip to back button above the card shows the presentation-style reverse side.

React with an emoji

Below every card is a row of six emojis — 👏 ❤️ 🔥 🎉 💪 😊. Tap one to add your reaction, tap again to remove it. Reactions are visible to everyone — and tapping a reaction count opens a small popover showing exactly who reacted.

Leave a comment

Click Comment on the right of the reaction bar to add a short note. Great for piling on a well-deserved thank-you, or sharing context the sender might not have known about.

Share a link

The Share button in the top-right of the card page copies a public link. Anyone at Access Group with the link can view the card — handy for Slack shout-outs or pinning in Teams channels.

07 · Notifications

Notifications

The bell icon in the top-right of every page holds your notifications. A red counter tells you how many you haven't read yet.

What you'll be notified about

  • Card received — someone sent you a recognition card.
  • Card edited — a sender updated a card you were given.
  • Card deleted — a card addressed to you was removed.
  • Reaction — someone reacted to a card you sent or received.
  • Comment — someone commented on a card you sent or received.

Clearing notifications

Click any notification to jump straight to the related card — that marks it read. Or click Mark all as read in the top-right of the dropdown to clear everything at once.

Tip. Unread notifications don't expire. If you're on leave for a week, they'll all be waiting when you return — no catch-up email needed.

08 · Leaderboard

The leaderboard

The leaderboard shows the most recognised people at Access Group each month. It's fun, friendly, and deliberately celebratory — not a competition.

How it works

  • The board counts cards received in the chosen month.
  • Use the Showing dropdown to switch between the current month and past snapshots.
  • The current month is labelled Live and updates in real time as cards are sent.
  • Past months are frozen snapshots — great for looking back on who shone in, say, March.
Why it matters. The leaderboard makes generosity visible. Sending more cards doesn't earn you a medal — but noticing great work around you does mean more colleagues feel seen.

09 · Your Profile

Your profile

Open My Profile in the sidebar to update how you show up in the app. There are four tabs: Profile, Security, Connected Accounts, and Preferences.

Profile

  • Profile Photo — click Upload photo. JPG, PNG, or WebP; the app auto-resizes to 200×200. If you signed up via Microsoft SSO, your Microsoft profile photo is pulled across automatically on first login — uploading here simply overrides it.
  • First / Last / Display Name — display name is optional and shown when present.
  • Phone & Position — visible on your profile card; useful for colleagues figuring out who does what.
  • Branch — ISO or Perth, set by an admin when your account is created.

Recognition history

Below the profile form is an All-time card showing your lifetime Cards Sent and Cards Received totals — these never reset, unlike the monthly counters on the dashboard.

Security

To change your password, enter your current one, then the new password twice. Passwords must be reasonably strong — the form will tell you if it's too short.

Connected Accounts

Click Connect on a row to link that account. Once connected, you can sign in with either method. To unlink, click Disconnect on the same row.

Preferences

Background Colour
Six options for the page background. Cream is the Access default; try Slate if you prefer a cooler look.
Card View
Physical Card (full branded preview) or Simple (compact list). Pick whichever scans faster for you.
Card Size
Compact, Normal, or Expanded — changes how much vertical space each card takes in your inbox.

10 · Tips & Help

Tips, FAQs & getting help

A grab-bag of the questions Staff users ask most in their first month with Access Recognition.

Writing a great card

  • Be specific. Name the project, the moment, the outcome. "Your push on Friday unblocked the whole pipeline" beats "great job".
  • Pick values on purpose. Ticking all five dilutes the meaning. Choose the one or two that really applied.
  • Recognise people outside your team. Cross-department thanks tend to mean the most — they're less expected.
  • Keep it authentic. A short, honest card beats a long, generic one every time.

Frequently asked questions

Can I send a card to myself?

No — the recipient picker won't let you pick yourself, and the app rejects the submission server-side if you try to sneak past the UI.

Can I delete a card I sent?

Yes. Open the card from your Sent tab and choose Delete. The recipient gets a small "card deleted" notification. Only the sender can delete.

Who can see my cards?

Your received and sent cards are visible to everyone at Access Group through the public feed and the card detail page, unless marked otherwise. This is by design — recognition is meant to be seen.

Do I get an email when I receive a card?

Not in v1 of the app — notifications live inside the bell icon. Email alerts are on the roadmap.

Something looks wrong / I can't sign in.

Try a refresh first. If that doesn't help, ping the HR team or the app's administrators — they have tools to reset accounts, restore deleted users, and investigate edge cases.

Where to find help

App URL

access.cgdev.site — bookmark it. Works on desktop and mobile.

Admins

Your HR team and department admins can reset your password, update your branch/department, or link your Microsoft account.

One last thing. This app only works if people use it. Every card you send makes someone's day, and a thriving culture of recognition starts with one person choosing to say thanks. That person might as well be you.
